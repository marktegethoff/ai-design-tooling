# Setup Guide

**Eyebrow:** Setup Guide

**Title:** Getting your team on the system

**Subtitle:** Sequence matters. Follow this order. Do not start with infrastructure.

---

## For AI Tooling leads

1. **Share the capability proof first**
   Run the case study at a design all-hands or team session before any tooling setup. Senior designers must see what the system produces before they'll engage with how it's structured.

2. **Run the vocabulary workshop**
   One session, 45 minutes. Introduce: behavioral contract, design intelligence infrastructure, brief (not prompt), direct/assisted/authored. End with a live demo of a CLAUDE.md edit changing Claude's behavior.

3. **Draft the org layer CLAUDE.md**
   3–5 rules. IP handling. Accessibility floor. Brand voice non-negotiables. Get legal review. This is the only document that requires that process — keep it thin so it clears quickly.

4. **Build a team CLAUDE.md template**
   One template per platform team. Import the org layer. Add team-specific sections. Include the decision hierarchy. Give teams the template at their first team session, not before — they need the vocabulary to use it well.

5. **Work with IT on the onboarding package**
   Define what needs to be pre-deployed: Claude Code installation, team CLAUDE.md, agent directory, skills directory. Provide a package spec; IT handles deployment via MDM. Target: zero terminal interaction for setup.

6. **Launch the commit ceremony**
   Start with a #design-decisions Slack channel. Give teams the entry template. Run it for 4 weeks before evaluating whether a git-backed memory sync is worth adding. The ritual must exist before the infrastructure matters.

---

> **Do not skip steps 1 and 2.**
> Teams that start with infrastructure setup (step 5) without the proof and vocabulary foundation (steps 1–2) treat the CLAUDE.md as a config file, skip the commit ceremony after two weeks, and conclude "it didn't really change how we work." The sequence is causal, not sequential.

---

## For individual designers

1. **Read your team CLAUDE.md before your first session**
   Not skim — read. Understand what each section governs. This is the brief you're working within.

2. **Add one personal context entry on day one**
   What are you working on? What decisions have you already made? What must not change? One paragraph at the bottom of your CLAUDE.md.

3. **Run your first real task, not a tutorial**
   Pick something from your actual work. The first session should produce something you can use. Tutorials don't build the habit — real work does.

4. **Contribute to the commit ceremony from session one**
   Even if your first entry is small. The habit is more important than the quality of early entries.
