# The Capability Proof

**Eyebrow:** Pillar 01

**Subtitle:** Senior designers respond to proof, not process documentation. The adoption hook is not a training deck — it is a real artifact built by a real designer using the real methodology.

---

> **The proof must be honest, not polished.**
> A case study that only shows what worked reads as marketing. The proof that moves senior designers shows where the methodology was required (not just helpful), where judgment was irreplaceable, and what the system couldn't solve. Honesty is what makes it credible.

---

## What the proof must demonstrate

**Designer in control**
The AI doing labor the designer directed — not the AI making decisions the designer accepted. Every output visible; every judgment owned.

**AI doing real labor**
Not summaries or text. Architecture decisions, code, design system components, structured analysis — work the designer would have found tedious or inaccessible alone.

**Output at the right quality bar**
The end product must meet the standard senior designers recognize as legitimate. A rough prototype proves a different (weaker) thing than a production-quality artifact.

---

## The on-ramp is required

Without an immediate next action, the proof converts attention into admiration, not adoption. Every proof asset must link directly to a workshop, a template, or a setup path. The designer finishes reading and has one clear thing to do next.
